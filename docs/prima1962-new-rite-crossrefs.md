# Prima 1962 New-Rite Cross-Reference Notes

These notes record searches made while correcting the standalone `/martyrology-prima1962/` page. They are reference notes only; no modern text is silently substituted into the 1962 Prima structure.

## Short Responsory

### Christe, Fili Dei vivi

- 1962 Prima text used here:
  - `℟. Christe, Fili Dei vivi, * miserére nobis.`
  - `℣. Qui sedes ad déxteram Patris.`
  - `℟. Miserére nobis.`
  - `℣. Glória Patri, et Fílio, * et Spirítui Sancto.`
  - `℟. Christe, Fili Dei vivi, * miserére nobis.`
- Correction: the `Iesu Christe, Fili Dei vivi` / `Jesu Christe, Fili Dei vivi` variants were present in the Divinum Officium source near a rubric for the Dominican use and are no longer included in the Roman Prima ordinary short responsory.
- New rite/Mass cross-reference: the Roman Missal contains a private prayer before Communion beginning `Domine Iesu Christe, Fili Dei vivi`; the Holy See GIRM, no. 268, explicitly identifies this prayer. This is not the same text or liturgical function as the Prima short responsory.
- Current Chinese working text:
  - `基督，永生天主之子，求你垂怜我们。`
  - `坐在圣父之右者。`
  - `垂怜我们。`
  - `愿光荣归于父、及子、及圣神。`

## Prima Collect

### Domine Deus omnipotens, qui ad principium huius diei...

- Search result: found as a traditional Prime prayer, not as a clear modern Liturgy of the Hours fixed text.
- Working Chinese is therefore marked as temporary translation:
  - `主、全能的天主，你使我们到达此日的开端；求你今天以你的德能保全我们，使我们在这一天不偏向任何罪恶，而使我们的言语、思想和行为，常常趋向实行你的正义。以上所求是靠我们的主基督。阿们。`

## Officium Capituli Prayer

### Dirigere et sanctificare...

- Search result: a secondary index reports `Dirigere et sanctificare...` in modern `Liturgia Horarum`, volumes III and IV, Morning Prayer ordinary section, pp. 1006-1007 and 970-971 respectively. This suggests a modern Latin new-rite source exists, but the project does not yet contain a verified Chinese text.
- Working Chinese is therefore marked as temporary translation:
  - `上主天主，天地君王，求你今天屈尊引导并圣化、统御并治理我们的心身、感官、言语和行为，使之合乎你的法律，并实行你的诫命；如此，世界的救主，赖你的助佑，我们今世及永世都堪当获得救恩与自由。你永生永王，世世无穷。阿们。`

## Ending Formulae

- `Adiutórium nostrum in nómine Dómini / Qui fecit cælum et terram`: common versicle-response, also used in many Roman blessings and rites.
- `Benedicite / Deus`: retained as old Office formula; no modern Chinese source was found in the project.
- `Dóminus nos benedícat...`: retained as old Office conclusion; no modern Chinese source was found in the project.
- `Fidélium ánimæ...`: retained as old Office prayer for the faithful departed; no modern Chinese source was found in the project.
